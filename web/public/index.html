Private messaging, built to outlast the quantum era

Logos is an end-to-end encrypted messenger with no phone number. Every conversation is sealed on your device — the relay only ever forwards unreadable envelopes, on the web and on iOS.

🛡️

Post-quantum key exchange

Sessions begin with PQXDH — a hybrid of classical X25519 and ML-KEM-1024. An attacker would have to break both, so messages captured today stay private against tomorrow's quantum computers.

🔒

End-to-end encrypted

A Double Ratchet rekeys every message with forward secrecy and post-compromise security, sealing it with ChaCha20-Poly1305. Only you and the person you're talking to can read it.

✉️

Sealed sender

The relay is minimal-trust: it stores public keys and sealed blobs, never plaintext, and sealed-sender certificates keep it from learning who is messaging whom.

📵

No phone number

Pick a username. No phone number, no email, no contact upload — your identity isn't tied to a SIM card.

🔑

Keys never leave your device

Identity and message keys are generated locally in your browser and stored only there. The server never sees a private key.

✅

Verify with safety numbers

Frequently asked questions

Is Logos really end-to-end encrypted?

Yes. All encryption happens on your device, and the relay only ever holds sealed, unreadable envelopes — it never sees message plaintext. Logos is still experimental and unaudited, so don't rely on it for high-stakes secrets yet.

Do I need a phone number?

No. You pick a username — there's no phone number, no email, and no contact upload.

What is post-quantum key exchange?

Logos starts every session with PQXDH, a hybrid handshake combining classical X25519 with ML-KEM-1024. Breaking it requires breaking both, so messages captured today stay private even against a future quantum computer — defeating "harvest now, decrypt later."

Can I message people on the Logos iOS app?

Yes. The web client and the iOS app share the same network — look up any Logos username, verify the safety number, and start chatting.

What if I lose my recovery secret?

Your identity can't be recovered. Keys live only on your device, so no one — including us — can reset your account. Keep your recovery secret somewhere safe.
